Why You Should Care About Your Privacy in This Era

June 09, 2025

Privacy isn't about hiding something wrong. It's about maintaining the space to be human.

In the digital age, every click, every search, every pause is recorded. We've normalized surveillance to the point where privacy feels like a luxury rather than a fundamental right.

The Real Cost of "Free" Services

When you're not paying for the product, you are the product. Your data, your behavior patterns, your relationships—all commodified and sold to the highest bidder.

Simple Steps to Reclaim Your Privacy

  • Use privacy-focused browsers like Firefox or Brave
  • Switch to search engines like DuckDuckGo or SearX
  • Use encrypted messaging apps like Signal
  • Consider a VPN for network traffic encryption
  • Host your own services when possible

The Wired Connection

Like Lain, we exist simultaneously in the physical and digital realms. But unlike her omnipresence, we can choose our level of integration. Privacy is about maintaining agency over that choice.

Remember: surveillance capitalism thrives on your data. Every small step toward privacy is an act of digital self-defense.

"The border between self and others is disappearing. But it doesn't have to disappear completely."
